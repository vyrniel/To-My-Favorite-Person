This is the story of how we got here. Not perfect. Not cinematic. Just real.

The Night I Remembered

It was years ago. A quiet night. I was alone, scrolling through Instagram, half distracted by nothing in particular.

A friend sent me a screenshot of a conversation he'd had with someone. When I saw the name Khushi, something stirred. A pull I couldn't place.

Familiarity before understanding.

I searched the name. Her profile appeared. Then her photo.

The recognition was immediate and silent. Not longing. Recognition.

Sixth Grade

We were in the same school during sixth grade. I rarely attended then. Most days I found reasons not to go.

One morning on the bus, a friend mentioned a new girl named Khushi. I barely registered it.

Later that day, during break, I saw her on the school ground. She was standing with her friends, smiling naturally at something one of them said.

I didn't talk to her. I didn't know her personally. There was no introduction. No shared glance. No interaction at all.

But the noise faded briefly. The moment felt unusually calm.

It passed. But it stayed.

This wasn't love. It wasn't a beginning. Just a moment quietly remembered.

Time Passing

I left that school not long after. Changed environments. Grew up unevenly.

Life became heavy. Confusing. Directionless at times. Some years felt like static. Others felt like waiting for something I couldn't name.

But the memory of her remained untouched. Not obsessed over. Just preserved.

Quiet. Unchanged. There.

Finding You Again

That night, years later, when your name reappeared on my screen, the recognition became clear.

I understood why that memory stayed. Why it never faded when so much else did.

It wasn't dramatic. It was grounding. Like finding something I didn't know I'd been carrying.

The Messy Middle

There was jealousy. Fear of losing something I hadn't earned yet. Actions driven by insecurity that I'm not proud of.

I don't justify them. I own them.

Growth doesn't come from pretending those moments didn't happen. It comes from acknowledging them and choosing differently.

How We Actually Began

It felt human. Not scripted. Not cinematic.

Just real.
